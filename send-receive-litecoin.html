How to Send and Receive Litecoin

This is where Litecoin becomes real. Learn how to receive Litecoin into your wallet, send it safely, verify the address, and avoid the common mistakes that trip people up.

Start the guide Back to guides

Step-by-step sending and receiving

1

Tap receive in your wallet

Your wallet will show a Litecoin address and usually a QR code. This is what someone uses to send Litecoin to you.

2

Copy the address carefully

Use the copy button when possible. Wallet addresses are long, so copying is better than typing by hand.

Best habit: always compare the first and last few characters after pasting an address.
3

Send a small test amount first

Before moving a larger amount, test with a small payment. This helps you learn the flow and catch any mistakes safely.

4

Use the send function to pay

Paste the address or scan the QR code, enter the amount, and double-check the destination before confirming.

5

Review the transaction details

Confirm the wallet address, the amount, and the fee. Once a Litecoin transaction is sent, you usually cannot reverse it.

6

Wait for confirmation

Your wallet will show the payment as pending first, then confirmed after the network processes it.

What to watch for

  • Double-check the address every time
  • Use QR codes when possible to reduce mistakes
  • Send a test amount first for bigger transfers
  • Understand that confirmed payments are generally final

Good beginner routine

  • Receive a small amount
  • Send a tiny test payment back out
  • Watch how long confirmation takes
  • Repeat until the process feels normal

Common questions

Things beginners ask all the time

What if I send to the wrong address?

That is why checking the address matters so much. Start with small test amounts until this feels natural.

How long does it take?

Wallets usually show the payment quickly, then it moves from pending to confirmed after network confirmations.

Why use a QR code?

QR codes reduce typing mistakes and make in-person Litecoin payments much easier.
